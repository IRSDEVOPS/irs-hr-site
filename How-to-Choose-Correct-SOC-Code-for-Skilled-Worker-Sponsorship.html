Choosing the Correct SOC Code for Skilled Workers

The SOC Code sets the foundation for every sponsored role. One mistake can lead to rejected Certificates of Sponsorship (CoS), visa refusals, and UKVI compliance audits. Protect your licence with IRS HR.

  • IRS HR ensures 100% compliance before submission.

The Dual Salary Requirement

To sponsor a Skilled Worker, you cannot just pick any salary. The UKVI requires employers to pay the higher of two specific minimums. Understanding this is crucial.

1. The Standard Rate

This is the absolute salary floor below which a person cannot be paid under this visa route.

Current General Standard: £41,700

*Unless a lower rate applies, such as the new entrant rate.

2. The Going Rate

This is the market salary specific to the individual SOC code assigned to the job role.

The Golden Rule: Pay whichever is HIGHER

A common mistake is paying the standard rate when the SOC going rate is actually higher.

4 Fatal SOC Code Mistakes

Avoid these common pitfalls that trigger immediate UKVI rejections and put your Sponsor Licence at risk.

Matching by Job Title Only

Selecting a code based purely on the job title rather than the actual daily responsibilities. UKVI assesses the job description, not just the title. If they decide another code is more applicable, your CoS will be rejected.

Ignoring Applicant History

Using a SOC code that the specific applicant doesn't qualify for based on whether they already hold a Skilled Worker visa. Transitional rules (Tables 1a, 2) apply strictly based on their initial sponsorship date.

Salary Shopping (Licence Revocation Risk)

Deliberately choosing a less accurate SOC code simply because it has a lower minimum salary threshold. If UKVI discovers this during an audit, it can lead to Sponsor Licence revocation and visa cancellations.

CoS vs. Interview Inconsistencies

Writing generic CoS descriptions copied from the internet. If the CoS description doesn't match what the applicant actually does, or how they describe their role during a Home Office interview, the visa will be refused.

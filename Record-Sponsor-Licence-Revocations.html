1. The Surge in Licence Revocations

Sponsor licence revocations—where the Home Office forcibly withdraws an organisation’s legal right to employ overseas nationals—have reached terrifying new heights in consecutive quarters.

Q4 2025 saw 1,516 Skilled Worker licence revocations, setting an unprecedented quarterly record. However, that record was immediately broken just three months later, with Q1 2026 seeing 1,545 revocations.

The Hidden Story in the Transparency Data

The Home Office's main statistics release often glosses over what is actively happening to employers currently sponsoring overseas workers. However, the official migration transparency data published alongside it exposes a severe administrative crackdown.

  • Failing to report changes in worker circumstances via the SMS.
  • Failing genuine vacancy checks during unannounced Home Office visits.
  • Discrepancies in Right to Work checks.

2026 Home Office UKVI Enforcement Alert

Avoid these 4 Mistakes
To Protect Your Sponsor Licence

Home Office UKVI raids are up 81%. A single administrative slip-up triggers a total ban and a 12-month lockout. Is your business actually protected?

The 4 Mistakes That Trigger Revocation

Here are the most common pitfalls found during Home Office UKVI audits and investigations this year.

01

The "Figurehead" AO Fallacy

If your Authorising Officer (AO) doesn't use the SMS and can't answer compliance questions during an audit, you're failing. UKVI expects the AO to be active, not just a name on a form.

Stop: Don't let your AO become a compliance stranger.
02

The "10-Day" Deadline Trap

Salary bumps, promotions, or location changes must be reported within 10 working days. Waiting for the month-end payroll run is the fastest way to get a "B-rating" downgrade.

Stop: Don't wait for payroll cycles to update the SMS.
03

The "Fragmented Records" Mess

If your HR system says "London" but the SMS says "Manchester," auditors see negligence. Without a single Golden Record, your audit trail is broken before the inspector even arrives.

Stop: Don't let disconnected spreadsheets run your compliance.
04

Blind Absence Monitoring

You are legally required to flag 10 consecutive days of unauthorised absence for sponsored workers. If your generic HR portal doesn't alert you automatically, you've already lost oversight.

Stop: Don't treat sponsored worker attendance like everyone else's.

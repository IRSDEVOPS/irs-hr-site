Sponsor Salary Compliance

As a Sponsor Licence holder, you must navigate two sets of laws: UK Labour Law (ACAS) and Home Office Immigration Rules. You must always pay the higher of the two figures.

The "Golden Rule" of Sponsorship

Never assume the "Going Rate" for a visa allows you to pay below the National Minimum Wage. If the Home Office salary threshold for a specific job code is lower than the National Minimum Wage for the hours worked, the National Minimum Wage takes precedence by law.

Effective April 1st

What's happening in 2026?

Following recommendations from the Low Pay Commission, the UK minimum wage is increasing significantly to ensure wages keep pace with living costs. These changes apply from the first full pay period after April 1, 2026.

Impact on Sponsorship

Many "Going Rates" for skilled worker codes (SOC) are currently below £12.71/hr.

Example: If a job's going rate is £11.90/hr, you can no longer pay that amount after April 2026. You must increase it to £12.71/hr to remain compliant with UK law.

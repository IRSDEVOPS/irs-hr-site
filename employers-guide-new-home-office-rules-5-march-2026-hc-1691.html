UK Immigration Rules Update

The Employer’s Guide to new Home Office rules published on 5 March 2026 HC 1691

The UK government has published a new "Statement of Changes in Immigration Rules" (HC 1691), introducing several significant updates that directly affect UK businesses, sponsors, and the wider workforce strategy.

For employers, these changes bring new compliance requirements for payroll, higher bars for talent recruitment, and specific restrictions on international hiring.

Below is a breakdown of what these updates mean for your business operations.

1. New Skilled Worker Payroll Compliance (Effective April 8, 2026)

One of the most critical changes for HR and payroll departments is the formalization of how salary compliance is measured for sponsored workers.

  • Pay Period Equality: Employers must ensure that the salary paid to a sponsored worker in every individual pay period meets or exceeds the required hourly "going rate" for that specific role.
  • Averaging Rules: Limited averaging is allowed to accommodate fluctuations. For monthly payrolls, salary over any three-month period must equal at least one-quarter of the required annual salary. For more frequent payrolls, a 12-week averaging window applies.
  • Irregular Hours: If you sponsor workers with uneven weekly hours, you must now explicitly confirm the working pattern. Compliance will be measured over a 17-week averaging period.
  • Why it matters: This makes it harder for employers to use "lumpy" pay (such as large quarterly bonuses) to satisfy the annual salary threshold if the base pay in a single month falls below the pro-rated minimum.

2. Increased English Language Bar for Settlement (Effective March 2027)

While this change doesn't hit until next year, it significantly impacts long-term workforce planning and employee retention strategies.

  • The Shift: The English language requirement for Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) is increasing from level B1 (Intermediate) to B2 (Upper Intermediate).
  • Affected Routes: This applies to most economic migration routes, including Skilled Worker, Global Talent, Scale-up, and UK Ancestry.
  • Business Impact: Employees currently on track for settlement may need additional support or training to meet this higher standard. If they fail to meet B2, they may be unable to settle, potentially leading to a loss of key talent.

3. Specific Hiring Restrictions (Effective March 26, 2026)

Businesses should be aware of new restrictions targeting specific nationalities for entry clearance (applying from outside the UK):

  • Afghanistan: Nationals can no longer apply for entry clearance under the Skilled Worker route.
  • Student Hiring Pipeline: Nationals of Afghanistan, Cameroon, Myanmar, and Sudan can no longer apply for Student visas. This may impact your graduate recruitment pipelines if you typically hire from these regions.
  • Note: These restrictions apply only to new applications from outside the UK. Individuals already in the UK on these routes are generally navigating extensions and are usually not affected by these specific entry restrictions.

4. Sector-Specific Updates: Prison Officers & Sports
  • Prison Service: "Prison service officers" have been added to the Skilled Occupations list with a time-limited lower salary threshold until 2028 to address staffing shortages.
  • Sports: The list of approved Sports Governing Bodies has been updated (notably adding the British Horseracing Authority), which is vital for businesses in the sports and betting sectors that recruit international athletes or trainers.

5. Global Talent: New Design Industry Route

The "Global Talent" visa is being expanded to include a dedicated track for the Design Industry. This is excellent news for creative agencies and tech firms looking to hire world-class designers without the burden of sponsorship.

  • Evidence Requirements: Applicants must show media recognition, international awards, or a track record of "outstanding" work.
  • Recommendation Letters: Candidates will need letters from well-established design organizations (at least one must be UK-based).
  • The Benefit: Once endorsed, these individuals can work for any employer (or be self-employed) without the administrative costs and duties associated with a Sponsor Licence.

6. Visitor Rule Changes (Impact on Business Travel)

If your business frequently hosts partners or consultants from overseas:

  • Visa Requirements: Citizens of Nicaragua and St Lucia now require a visa to visit the UK for business meetings or training.
  • Transit: The "Transit Without Visa" scheme has been removed from the rules, meaning business travelers passing through the UK to other destinations may now face different requirements.

Key Deadlines for Businesses
  • March 5, 2026: Immediate visa requirements for Nicaragua and St Lucia.
  • March 26, 2026: Nationality-based restrictions for Skilled Workers and Students begin.
  • April 8, 2026: New Skilled Worker salary compliance and pay period rules take effect.
  • March 26, 2027: The B2 English language requirement for settlement begins.

Lesson 1.2: The RFI Sprint

When a discrepancy is flagged, the Home Office issues a Request for Further Information (RFI). You now have a strict 5-working-day window to provide comprehensive evidence. Relying on paper files or disjointed spreadsheets guarantees failure during this sprint.

Action Item

Audit your current payroll immediately. Select three current sponsored workers and cross-reference their last month's gross pay against the exact figure stated on their active Certificate of Sponsorship. Identify any variance down to the penny.

Module 4

The Flawless Application Suite

Building the visual and documentary evidence that Home Office caseworkers look for to approve allocations quickly.

Lesson 4.1: The Strategic Hierarchy Chart

An organizational chart is mandatory. However, a standard org chart is not enough. Your 2026 chart must explicitly highlight: 1) Existing settled workers, 2) Existing sponsored workers, and 3) The exact structural location of the "Proposed CoS Vacancy".

Lesson 4.2: SOC Code Alignment

With the revised Appendix-D terminology, matching your internal job descriptions to the official Standard Occupational Classification (SOC) codes is critical. Using outdated terminology or "creative" job titles will result in your application being rejected for not meeting skill-level requirements.

Action Item

Review your current HR hierarchy chart. Update it using color-coding to differentiate between settled staff, sponsored staff, and the specific vacant posts you calculated in Module 3.

Module 5

The Post-Grant Shield

Preventing licence revocation and avoiding director disqualification after the visa has been granted.

Lesson 5.1: The 10-Day SMS Window

Any change in a sponsored worker's circumstances (address change, promotion, salary increase, alternative work location) MUST be reported on the SMS within 10 working days. IRS HR automation is critical here, as manual tracking routinely misses this deadline.

Lesson 5.2: The Unpaid Leave Trap

If a worker takes unpaid leave or sick leave that drops their monthly pay below their CoS stated salary, the HMRC data link flags it as an illegal underpayment. You must use the SMS to report permitted absences *before* the payroll is submitted.

Action Item

Schedule a "Mock SMS Audit" for next Tuesday. Pick one employee, pretend they have moved house, and test how long it takes your internal system to recognize the change and draft an SMS update.

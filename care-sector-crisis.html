What is Driving the Wave of Revocations?

1. The "Prestwick" Precedent

The Court of Appeal ruling in Prestwick Care Ltd v SSHD [2025] confirmed that the Home Office does not need to consider the impact on staffing or service users before revoking a licence.

2. Intelligence-Led Monitoring

The Home Office no longer relies on site visits. They now use data-matching across HMRC/PAYE and the Sponsor Management System (SMS) to detect discrepancies remotely.

3. Zero Tolerance

"Technical" breaches like late reporting or weak absence monitoring—once minor issues—now lead to immediate suspension.

THE 4 FATAL COMPLIANCE FAILURES

Why Care Licences Are Lost

1

Salary & Hours Discrepancies

Salaries falling below thresholds due to deductions, or hours worked not matching the CoS.

2

Genuine Vacancy Failure

Roles described on the CoS do not exist in practice, or resemble "labour supply" to third parties.

3

Reporting Lag

Failure to report changes in job duties, salary, or work location within 10 working days.

4

Worker Protection Breaches

Recouping Immigration Skills Charge or CoS fees from workers (Strictly prohibited since Dec 2024).

CASE UPDATE: JAN 2026

OPERATION TORNADO VALIDATED

High Court Refuses Judicial Review in Migrants' Rights Network v SSHD [2026] EWHC 39 (Admin)

A defining moment for "Intelligence-Led" Immigration Enforcement.

⚖️ The Challenge

The Migrants' Rights Network (MRN) challenged the Home Office's "Operation Tornado"—a campaign of intensive raids. MRN argued that targeting specific sectors amounted to indirect discrimination and breached equality duties.

Claimant's Argument: "Raids disproportionately impact specific ethnic groups working in these sectors."

🛡️ The Defense

The Secretary of State (SSHD) argued that the operations were "intelligence-led." The targeting was based on data regarding illegal working prevalence, not race, making it a proportionate means to achieve immigration control.

Court's Ruling: "Permission to apply for judicial review is REFUSED."

High-Risk Sectors

"Operation Tornado" specifically targeted industries identified by Home Office intelligence as having high rates of illegal working. Businesses in these sectors face the highest risk of enforcement.

High Priority Enforcement Targets

The Court's Findings

Why the challenge failed and what it establishes as legal precedent.

1. Targeting is Lawful

The Court ruled that even if targeting specific sectors disproportionately affects certain groups, it is lawful if based on intelligence. Maintaining immigration control is a "legitimate aim."

2. PSED Satisfied

Existing Equality Impact Assessments (EIAs) were deemed sufficient. The Home Office does not need to conduct a fresh assessment for every new wave of operational intensification.

3. "Results" Matter

The claimant argued that not every raid yields arrests. The Court rejected this, stating that raids also serve as a deterrent, signaling that checks are active.

4. Limited Recourse

This sets a strong precedent. Small businesses in targeted sectors cannot successfully argue that a raid is unlawful simply based on discrimination grounds if intelligence exists.

The "Intelligence-Led" Cycle

4

Challenges based on discrimination are refused by the High Court.

Implications for Sponsors

This judgment effectively gives the Home Office a "Green Light" to ramp up enforcement. The graph illustrates the shift in legal weight: "Intelligence-Led Justification" now heavily outweighs "Discrimination Claims" in the eyes of the court for these specific operations.

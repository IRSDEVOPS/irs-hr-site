Why Burnham Kept Mahmood: The "Hardball" Home Secretary and Your Sponsor Licence

IRS HR Immigration Advisory

Corporate Compliance & Policy Team

Key Takeaways for Employers

  • Continuity of Enforcement: Andy Burnham retaining Shabana Mahmood signals a commitment to strict immigration control and rigorous corporate enforcement.
  • Right to Work Fines Tripled: The £60,000 per-worker fine for illegal working is being aggressively pursued alongside digital-only eVisa checks.
  • Sponsor Revocations Accelerating: The Home Office's data-driven crackdown shows no signs of slowing down, making mock audits essential.
  • ILR Stays Tough: Punishing Indefinite Leave to Remain rules, including the 15-year route for care workers, remain firmly in place.

When Andy Burnham assumed the premiership following Keir Starmer's sudden resignation, Westminster held its breath to see who would take the keys to the Home Office. The answer? Shabana Mahmood stays put.

For UK businesses relying on international talent, this decision is a loud, unambiguous klaxon. Burnham’s choice to retain Mahmood—a politician who has cultivated a fiercely "hardball" reputation on immigration—proves that the administration’s aggressive compliance crackdown was not just a Starmer-era phase. It is the permanent new reality.

The New Right to Work Checks & Crippling Fines

Perhaps the most immediate and dangerous threat to UK employers under Mahmood's continuing tenure is the weaponization of Right to Work (RTW) checks. The transition to a digital-by-default immigration system (eVisas) has given the Home Office unprecedented real-time oversight over your workforce.

Civil Penalties: £60,000 Per Worker

The penalty for employing someone without the correct immigration status (even due to an administrative oversight in recording a share code) has skyrocketed. Mahmood's department is strictly enforcing the tripled fine structure.

Mahmood’s Home Office is actively deploying task forces to conduct unannounced site visits. If your RTW records are not perfectly maintained in line with the new digital eVisa requirements, you face not only these crippling fines but the immediate loss of your sponsor licence.

Sponsor Revocations on the Rise

Mahmood’s retention guarantees that the aggressive data-sharing between HMRC (payroll) and the Home Office (Sponsor Management System) will intensify.

As we’ve previously reported, sponsor licence revocations have surged drastically. The days of the Home Office issuing "action plans" for minor discrepancies are over. If your SMS data does not perfectly match your PAYE data—whether it's a slight change in salary, working hours, or primary work location—your licence is instantly suspended.

In Q1 of 2026 alone, 1,535 licences were revoked. If this pace continues, 2026 will shatter all previous records for corporate immigration enforcement.

Burnham clearly views this tough enforcement as a political asset. By squeezing employers to reduce net migration organically, the government avoids the optics of changing visa caps while still drastically lowering numbers through compliance attrition.

Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR): No Relief in Sight

Migrants hoping that a Burnham-led government might soften the stringent rules surrounding Indefinite Leave to Remain will be disappointed. Mahmood's mandate includes holding the line on the UK's strict settlement criteria.

15-Year Care Worker Route

The deeply controversial extension requiring care workers to reside in the UK for 15 years before qualifying for ILR is here to stay, defended as necessary for "workforce stability."

High Salary Thresholds

The high income thresholds required for settlement remain untouched, continuing to price many skilled workers out of permanent residency under current conditions.

Automated Absence Tracking

Digital borders mean that the 180-day absence rule for ILR is tracked automatically and ruthlessly. Ignorance of exact travel dates is no longer a viable defense.

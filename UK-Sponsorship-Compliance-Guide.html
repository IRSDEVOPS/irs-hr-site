Everything You Need to Know About The New Home Office Guidance Update

6 March 2026

The Home Office has published the latest version of Appendix D that governs how you must maintain records for sponsored workers. IRS HR breaks down the complex Sponsor Licence guidance into an interactive, audit-ready toolkit.

Acceptable Formats

Records can be physical paper files or digital scans. They must simply be strictly organized and "available on request".

⏳

Retention Period

Keep documents for the entire duration of sponsorship, PLUS one year after employment ends.

🏢

License Documents

Original documents from your license application must be kept as long as you hold a license.

Document Retention Timeline

📅

Summary of Changes: 6 March 2026

Mandatory adjustments implemented to reflect the latest version (03/26) of the sponsor guidance.

01

Employment Rights Duty (Section 5l)

Sponsors must now keep evidence that they have made workers aware of their employment rights in the UK.

02

Seasonal Worker eVisa Shift

Applications on or after 25 Feb 2026 receive an eVisa only. Physical vignettes are no longer standard.

The 4 Pillars of Compliance

Pillar 1: Right to Work & Entry

Online Right to Work Check

Must use the 'Share Code' service. Retain the PDF result for your records.

Pillar 2: Recruitment Process

Copies of ads, URL screenshots, and notes explaining the candidate selection rationale are mandatory.

Pillar 3: Salary & Skills

Payslips, bank transfer proof, and detailed job descriptions must be readily available for audit.

Pillar 4: Admin Records

Contact history and absence logs must be kept up to date for every sponsored individual.
